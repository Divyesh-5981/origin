# DEV Weekend Challenge: Passion Edition — Full Guide

Here is the comprehensive breakdown of all the official details, rules, deadlines, and criteria for the **DEV Weekend Challenge: Passion Edition**. You can save this information directly as a `.md` file for easy reference over the weekend.

---

## 🎯 The Prompt

Build something **inspired by passion**.

Passion is the fire that drives us. It’s the rivalry that pushes competitors to greatness, the love that fuels late-night side projects, and the devotion fans pour into their teams, fandoms, or crafts. With the ongoing World Cup, passion for the game is everywhere.

### Inspiration & Angles

You can interpret this prompt in any way you like. Some suggested ideas include:

- A tribute to a rivalry (sporting or otherwise).
- A tool for tracking the projects, crafts, or hobbies you love.
- A World Cup companion app for truly devoted fans.
- Anything that captures obsession, romance, or team spirit.

## 🏆 Prizes & Categories

Five individual winners will be selected, each receiving:

- **$200 USD** cash prize
- **DEV++ Membership**
- An **exclusive DEV profile badge**

_Note: All participants who submit a valid entry will receive a completion badge._

### Prize Categories

There is one **Overall Winner** spot (open to any technology stack) and four specific **Prize Category Winners** based on partner tools.

- **Best use of [Snowflake](https://www.snowflake.com/en/)**
- **Best use of [Solana](https://solana.com/)**
- **Best use of [ElevenLabs](https://elevenlabs.io/)**
- **Best use of [Google AI](https://ai.google.dev/)**

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Prize category winners are drawn from a much smaller pool of submissions, meaning your odds of taking home a prize are meaningfully higher if you use one of these tools.
>
> **Multi-Tech Rule:** You are allowed to use multiple partner technologies within a single submission to qualify for multiple categories simultaneously. However, participants are strictly limited to **one win per challenge**.

---

## 📋 Judging Criteria

All submissions are evaluated based on five specific criteria points:

1.  **Relevance to Theme:** Does the project clearly and meaningfully connect to the challenge theme of passion?
2.  **Creativity:** Is the concept original, or does it showcase an interesting take on a familiar problem?
3.  **Technical Execution:** Is the project stable, functional, well-built, and easy for judges to navigate/use?
4.  **Writing Quality:** Is your accompanying submission post clearly written, engaging, and easy to follow?
5.  **Use of Prize Category Technology (Optional):** If you are pursuing a specific sponsor category, does your submission meaningfully and deeply incorporate that chosen technology?
